Evie T. Joselow, Ph.D. is an art historian, researcher and lecturer. She was awarded her Ph.D. in art history from The Graduate School of The City University of New York, May 1998, having received her B.A. in art history from Vassar College. Dr. Joselow served as the Chief of Research at the Commission for Art Recovery, an organization established to identify and locate art looted by the Nazis. She is now an independent consultant, specializing in provenance research and collection management issues for Museums and private collectors. Dr. Joselow also teaches design history at the New York School of Interior Design, and courses on New York City art and architecture at New York University. She has been the recipient of many research fellowships for study in the United States and abroad and has lectured and published widely on art and architectural subjects.
